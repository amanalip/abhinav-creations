# 13 - Content and Launch Governance

## 1. Why governance matters

A technically correct page can still harm a business if its price, legal policy, address, availability, testimonial, or service promise is wrong. Governance defines who may approve what.

## 2. Content lifecycle

~~~text
Proposed -> Source-verified -> Owner-approved -> Implemented
    -> QA-reviewed -> Published -> Periodically reviewed
~~~

“Implemented” does not automatically mean “approved for publication.”

## 3. Approval matrix

| Content | Suggested approver | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Business name and identity | Business owner | Registration/brand source |
| Address and contact routes | Owner/operations | Verified live details |
| Service specifications | Operations lead | Equipment/service record |
| Prices and taxes | Owner/finance | Approved rate sheet |
| Availability and booking rules | Operations | Provider configuration |
| Testimonials | Quoted person and owner | Written permission |
| Photographs/video | Rights owner | License or consent |
| Privacy and legal policies | Qualified owner/adviser | Approved dated text |
| Accessibility claims | Qualified audit evidence | Test report |

This is operational guidance, not legal advice.

## 4. Current pre-launch dependencies

The root checklist identifies pending business claims, pricing, booking, contact details, GSTIN, legal text, media, and launch approval. These must remain visibly pending until supplied and approved.

## 5. Anti-fabrication rules

- Never infer a price from competitors.
- Never invent testimonials.
- Never turn aspirational equipment into confirmed inventory.
- Never generate binding policy language and present it as approved legal text.
- Never assume an email or phone number.
- Never imply live availability without a connected provider.
- Date approved source material.

## 6. Change record

For material content, record:

- Exact approved wording.
- Approver.
- Approval date.
- Source reference.
- Pages affected.
- Review/expiry date.

Avoid storing private personal evidence in a public repository.

## 7. Launch gate

- [ ] Every visible claim has an owner.
- [ ] Pending labels are accurate.
- [ ] Contact route was tested.
- [ ] Prices and taxes match approved source.
- [ ] Booking status is truthful.
- [ ] Media rights are recorded.
- [ ] Policy pages contain approved text.
- [ ] Sitemap matches public pages.
- [ ] Live QA passed.
- [ ] Maintenance owner accepted handoff.

## 8. Sanity check

This guide does not approve any business or legal content. It describes the process needed to obtain and preserve approval.
